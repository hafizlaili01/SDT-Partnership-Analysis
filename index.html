Commercial Engagement & Revenue Models

The MIMOS-SDT partnership is a highly strategic, commercially-focused collaboration. The global quantum computing market is projected to reach $125 billion by 2030, with the Asia-Pacific region expected to be the fastest-growing market at a 40% compound annual growth rate.[8] This collaboration is designed to capitalize on that growth through several key, commercially viable revenue streams for both parties.

SDT Inc. Revenue Streams

  • Quantum-as-a-Service (QaaS): As a key provider in the joint venture, SDT can offer cloud-based access to the quantum computing resources at the Quantum Intelligence Centre. This "pay-as-you-go" or subscription model lowers the barrier to entry for businesses, allowing them to run quantum algorithms and solutions without needing to invest in expensive hardware.[9, 10] The QIC is designed to serve as a hybrid data center for specialized tasks, such as solving complex problems in finance, AI, and biotechnology.[3, 10]
  • Hardware Sales & Licensing: SDT's core business as a quantum-grade Original Design Manufacturer (ODM) allows it to generate revenue by exporting and selling its ultra-precision quantum measurement and control equipment, like the Qubit Controller Unit (QCU), to other research institutions and companies across Asia.[1] This also includes the potential to sell full-stack quantum computers.[11]
  • Professional Services & Consulting: SDT can leverage its deep-tech expertise to provide specialized consulting services to clients. This model allows the company to generate revenue by offering technical knowledge and implementation support to enterprises looking to integrate quantum solutions into their industrial settings and digital transformation initiatives.[1, 12]

MIMOS Berhad Revenue Streams

  • Technology Licensing & Commercialization: As a government-backed national R&D center, MIMOS's primary commercial model is to monetize intellectual property developed at the QIC. This involves licensing new quantum-related technologies to private firms and fostering the creation of new spin-off companies, which aligns with its goal of creating a "Quantum Valley" ecosystem.[13, 14]
  • Talent Development & Training: MIMOS can generate significant revenue by offering specialized, hands-on industrial upskilling programs to train the next generation of quantum researchers and engineers. This service is crucial for meeting the future workforce demands of the quantum industry and is a key part of MIMOS's mission.[15, 16]
  • Shared Facilities Access: MIMOS has a business model of providing access to its world-class facilities to industry partners and academia.[15] The newly established QIC can be a key revenue driver, allowing external companies to use the quantum hardware, labs, and equipment for their own research and development projects for a fee.
